Saving places to your trip

Three ways to drop a place into your 🗺 Trip planner:

  • Custom waypoint — open any day → 📍 Custom, then type lat/lng, tap Use my GPS, or paste a map link.
  • Paste a link — Google or Apple Maps links, a geo: link, or just “lat, lng”. The app reads the coordinates for you.
  • Share from Maps (Android) — in Google Maps tap Share and pick AuraGold. It opens with the place ready to add.

iPhone / iPad: Apple doesn't let installed web apps appear in the iOS share sheet (as of 2026), so “Share → AuraGold” won't show. Use Paste a link instead — copy the address from Maps and paste it into the Custom waypoint screen.

Short links (maps.app.goo.gl/…) can't be read offline. Open the short link in your browser first, then copy the full URL from the address bar and paste that.

🏠 Home calibration session

Teach the classifier with known targets at home — before the trip. Start a session, swing your detector over a sample, then tap what it was. Each tap saves the last 5 s of audio, GPS-tagged and labelled.

🎒 What to bring — a gold ring or chain, a gold-plated coin, a copper coin, steel nails, a bottle cap, a brass cartridge case, and a known hot rock or piece of red dirt. Aim for 10+ examples of each class.

🧠 Smart classifier

On-device classifier learns from your labels. A separate model is trained per detector + coil (their audio & ground coverage differ). Run Home Calibration with known gold + junk before the trip. All data stays on this device — nothing uploads.

Pipeline smoke-test: load 48 synthetic clips (12 per class) to verify feature extraction → train → infer → export end-to-end before your trip. Not real detector audio — clear them when done. (They train the Gold Monster model.)

🌙 Detection Window

Forecasts per-hour detector efficiency for the next 24 h from your location — solar/geomagnetic & grid EMI, atmosphere, ground coupling, moonlight and powerline proximity. A first-of-its-kind, fully heuristic estimate. Open the full chart from ☰ Menu → 🌙 Best times today or the badge at the top of the map.

Advanced: detection-model weights

How much each factor counts toward the score. Defaults are conservative — nudge them to match your detector & ground, then re-open the chart. Weights renormalise to 100%.

Live inputs (NOAA space weather, weather, AEMO grid demand) are cached for offline use; solar & moon are computed on-device and always work with no signal.

Data sources

The base maps, 🎯 Nugget Potential heatmaps and nugget model are built from these open datasets:

  • SRTM — 30 m elevation (NASA)
  • VicMine — Victorian Government mineral occurrence points
  • GA magnetics — Geoscience Australia TMI-RTP magnetic grid
  • GA radiometrics — Geoscience Australia K/Th channels
  • Vic geology — Geological Survey of Victoria 1:250k surface geology
  • Fire — DELWP fire history + Sentinel-2 NBR burn severity
  • Esri — basemap tiles

The 🎯 Nugget Potential heatmap is a heuristic model over these layers — a guide to promising ground, not a guarantee of gold.

🌙 Best times today

–/100
now+12 h+24 h

Tap any bar for the hour-by-hour “why”. Shaded bars are after dark. Scores are heuristic estimates, not guarantees.

How it works. Each hour is scored 0–100 from five factors — grid/solar EMI (35%), atmosphere (20%, thermal transitions at dawn/dusk), ground coupling (20%, recent rain & temperature), visibility (10%, daylight or moonlight), and powerline proximity (15%). A 20–25% lift over the daily average is a real, workable edge — and yes, that can mean a night shift. Tune the weights in ⚙ Settings → Detection Window. Calibrate further by labelling confirmed-gold events in 🎵 Audio events.

Miner's Right

You must hold and carry a current Victorian Miner's Right whenever you prospect. It costs $28.60 and lasts 10 years. Kids under 18 are covered if they're with an adult who holds one.

Where you can fossick (with a Miner's Right)

✓ Allowed

  • State forests — the main one
  • Unreserved Crown land
  • Regional Parks (e.g. Hepburn, Creswick)
  • Designated prospecting areas inside some national / state / historic / heritage parks — only in the marked area (grab the Parks Vic PDF)
  • Private land with the owner's written permission
  • Inside an Exploration Licence (EL) on otherwise-open Crown / State Forest — an EL does not lock you out

✗ Off-limits

  • National / State Parks outside the designated areas
  • Nature Conservation Reserves & Reference Areas (except designated blocks)
  • Mining / Prospecting / Retention Licences (MIN, ML, PL, RL) — need the holder's consent
  • Rivers & creeks on the exempt list
  • Water-supply catchments, Aboriginal heritage & historic mining-relic sites

So it's not only State Forest — but State Forest + Crown land is the simplest "yes". When in doubt, it's a no until GeoVic says otherwise.
