Saving places to your trip

Three ways to drop a place into your 🗺 Trip planner:

  • Custom waypoint — open any day → 📍 Custom, then type lat/lng, tap Use my GPS, or paste a map link.
  • Paste a link — Google or Apple Maps links, a geo: link, or just “lat, lng”. The app reads the coordinates for you.
  • Share from Maps (Android) — in Google Maps tap Share and pick AuraGold. It opens with the place ready to add.

iPhone / iPad: Apple doesn't let installed web apps appear in the iOS share sheet (as of 2026), so “Share → AuraGold” won't show. Use Paste a link instead — copy the address from Maps and paste it into the Custom waypoint screen.

Short links (maps.app.goo.gl/…) can't be read offline. Open the short link in your browser first, then copy the full URL from the address bar and paste that.

🪙 Fossicking checklist

Miner's Right

You must hold and carry a current Victorian Miner's Right whenever you prospect. It costs $28.60 and lasts 10 years. Kids under 18 are covered if they're with an adult who holds one.

Buy / renew a Miner's Right →

Where you can fossick (with a Miner's Right)

✓ Allowed

  • State forests — the main one
  • Unreserved Crown land
  • Regional Parks (e.g. Hepburn, Creswick)
  • Designated prospecting areas inside some national / state / historic / heritage parks — only in the marked area (grab the Parks Vic PDF)
  • Private land with the owner's written permission
  • Inside an Exploration Licence (EL) on otherwise-open Crown / State Forest — an EL does not lock you out

✗ Off-limits

  • National / State Parks outside the designated areas
  • Nature Conservation Reserves & Reference Areas (except designated blocks)
  • Mining / Prospecting / Retention Licences (MIN, ML, PL, RL) — need the holder's consent
  • Rivers & creeks on the exempt list
  • Water-supply catchments, Aboriginal heritage & historic mining-relic sites

So it's not only State Forest — but State Forest + Crown land is the simplest "yes". When in doubt, it's a no until GeoVic says otherwise.
